Inquiry Part 2 — Planning the First Settlement
Before a nation can rise, its people must decide where to place their first markers, how to use their tools, and where early settlements should begin.
1. Where would you place your first settlement, and why would that location matter?
2. Where would you place your first road or pathway so people, goods, and information can move safely?
3. Where would you place your first market or exchange area, and what would make that location useful?
4. What tools would your people need first, and what work would those tools help them do?
5. What markers would you place first to help define routes, safe zones, resource areas, or community boundaries?
6. Which area would you protect first so your early settlements can survive and grow?
These answers will shape how your people move from observation into organization.
