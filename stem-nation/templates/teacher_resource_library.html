STEM Nation · Teacher Resource Engine

Teacher Resource Library

The Teacher Resource Library is the future educator-side instructional workspace for organizing, attaching, aligning, and exporting civilization-based learning resources.

Instructional Resource Engine

STEM Nation is designed to support customizable teacher-driven instruction. Educators should eventually be able to upload, organize, edit, attach, and align instructional materials directly to simulation experiences.

Prototype Architecture · Upload System Not Yet Active

Planned Resource Categories

Lesson Plans

Editable pacing guides, objectives, discussion prompts, extensions, and facilitation notes.

Primary Sources

First-hand accounts, speeches, letters, treaties, interviews, and historical evidence.

Archives & Government Records

National Archives materials, congressional records, treaties, hearings, court records, public laws, government reports, census records, and official historical documents.

Maps & Geography

Cartographic material, trade routes, regional comparisons, environmental analysis, and migration maps.

Media & Visuals

Images, documentary clips, oral histories, visual evidence, audio, and presentation media.

Assessment Materials

Rubrics, reflection prompts, discussion checkpoints, evidence trackers, and archive evaluation tools.

Planned Teacher Workflow

1. Upload or Create

Teachers upload or create instructional resources tied to a civilization topic or simulation phase.

2. Tag & Organize

Resources are tagged by week, module, theme, civilization focus, standards alignment, and artifact type.

3. Attach to Simulation

Teachers attach selected resources to student experiences such as Baseline Inquiry, Strategic Map Defense, or Survival Planning.

4. Align & Export

Future systems help align resources to district/state standards and export teacher/student classroom packets.

Security & Storage Planning

Real uploads are intentionally not active yet. Before enabling teacher uploads, STEM Nation must establish secure storage architecture, authentication systems, file ownership rules, privacy controls, and classroom/district separation policies.

Future upload systems may include PDFs, DOCX files, images, maps, audio/video references, and teacher-created instructional packets.
