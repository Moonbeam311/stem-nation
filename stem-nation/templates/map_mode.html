Settlement Region Reference Board

Use these possible starting zones as your reference. Your final answer should explain which region gives your people the strongest beginning and what problem they must overcome.

River Corridor

Possible Advantage: fresh water, farming, fishing, travel, trade routes.

Possible Risk: flooding, disease, competition from other groups.

Best For: food production and early population growth.

Forest Edge

Possible Advantage: wood, shelter, animals, tools, concealment.

Possible Risk: limited visibility, harder movement, hidden danger.

Best For: building materials and protected early survival.

Highland Ridge

Possible Advantage: defense, visibility, protection from flooding.

Possible Risk: less farming land, harder travel, exposure to weather.

Best For: security and watching movement across the land.

Coastal Bay

Possible Advantage: fishing, travel, trade, contact with other groups.

Possible Risk: storms, exposure, invasion routes, saltwater limits.

Best For: exchange, movement, and maritime growth.

Open Plain

Possible Advantage: farming space, expansion, visibility, herd movement.

Possible Risk: weak natural defense, drought, exposure.

Best For: agriculture, population spread, and future city growth.

Balanced Crossroads

Possible Advantage: access to several resources and movement routes.

Possible Risk: more competition, harder to defend, divided priorities.

Best For: trade, diplomacy, and flexible development.

Student Task:
Identify your chosen region, explain its strongest advantage, name its greatest risk, and describe how your people will survive the first stage of civilization.

1. Observe the Land

What did the map reveal? Look for water, food sources, movement paths, barriers, and dangerous terrain.

2. Identify Resources

Decide which resources matter first: food, shelter, defense, trade access, or room to grow.

3. Name the Risk

Every region has weakness. Explain the challenge your people must overcome to survive.

4. Defend the Decision

Use evidence from the map to justify your settlement choice. Strong leaders explain their reasoning.
