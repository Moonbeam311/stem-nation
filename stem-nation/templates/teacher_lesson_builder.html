Teacher Lesson Builder

The Teacher Lesson Builder is the future educator-side authoring system for creating, modifying, organizing, and customizing civilization-based lessons connected to the STEM Nation simulation.

Lesson Authoring Philosophy

STEM Nation lessons are designed around inquiry, evidence, systems thinking, consequence, and reflection. Teachers should eventually be able to customize pacing, prompts, artifacts, facilitation guidance, and instructional framing around simulation experiences.

Lesson Structure

Step-by-Step Teaching Sequence

STEM Nation lessons are designed as guided classroom experiences. Teachers should eventually be able to customize and follow a structured instructional sequence that supports inquiry, discussion, evidence usage, artifact creation, and consequence-based reasoning.

1. Launch / Hook

Introduce the civilization challenge, geography problem, historical framing, or inquiry question that begins the lesson experience.

2. Baseline Inquiry / Observation

Students observe maps, identify risks/resources, and record initial observations before major decisions are made.

3. Teacher Pause & Probe

Teacher pauses the simulation flow to compare responses, surface assumptions, and deepen reasoning through guided questioning.

4. Student Decision Workspace

Students collaborate, debate, and develop strategic decisions connected to settlement, survival, trade, or governance.

5. Evidence Challenge

Students defend decisions using maps, primary sources, attached resources, or historical evidence.

6. Archive Creation

Students create Founding Archives, Survival Archives, or other artifact records documenting reasoning and consequence analysis.

7. Reflection & Discussion

Teacher facilitates consequence discussion, systems thinking, comparison between civilizations, and reflection on tradeoffs.

8. Export / Packet Collection

Teacher reviews artifacts, archive exports, and packet materials connected to the lesson sequence.

9. Extension / Next Lesson Bridge

Transition students into future lessons involving survival, trade, diplomacy, governance, expansion, or conflict.
