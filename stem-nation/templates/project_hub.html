Rubric Guidelines for Teachers and Students

These rubric guidelines explain what strong civilization reasoning looks like during Week 1 settlement analysis and discussion.

Observation Quality

4 · Strong — Explains multiple terrain relationships and predicts consequences clearly.
3 · Proficient — Identifies major terrain features with reasonable explanation.
2 · Developing — Identifies terrain but explanation is limited.
1 · Beginning — Gives unclear or minimal observations.
Example Response: "The river supports farming and transportation, but flooding could destroy early settlements if defenses are weak."

Evidence Use

4 · Strong — Uses map evidence directly to support reasoning.
3 · Proficient — Gives evidence connected to the decision.
2 · Developing — Gives limited or incomplete evidence.
1 · Beginning — Gives little or no supporting evidence.
Example Response: "I chose the coast because the water routes can increase trade and movement between civilizations."

Risk Awareness

4 · Strong — Identifies long-term risks and tradeoffs clearly.
3 · Proficient — Identifies major dangers connected to the region.
2 · Developing — Identifies a danger but explanation is incomplete.
1 · Beginning — Shows little awareness of consequences or risk.
Example Response: "The plains support farming, but open land makes invasion and defense more difficult."

Settlement Logic

4 · Strong — Gives a clear survival strategy connected to geography and growth.
3 · Proficient — Explains a reasonable settlement choice.
2 · Developing — Gives a basic strategy with limited reasoning.
1 · Beginning — Gives unclear settlement reasoning.
Example Response: "The highlands provide natural defense, so our civilization can protect resources while expanding slowly."

Systems Thinking

4 · Strong — Connects food, trade, defense, movement, and growth together.
3 · Proficient — Connects multiple systems with reasonable explanation.
2 · Developing — Shows limited connections between systems.
1 · Beginning — Sees decisions in isolation with little systems awareness.
Example Response: "Trade routes increase wealth and communication, but they also expose the civilization to conflict and invasion."

Collaboration / Discussion

4 · Strong — Builds on ideas, asks questions, and contributes thoughtfully.
3 · Proficient — Participates consistently in discussion.
2 · Developing — Participates inconsistently or with limited explanation.
1 · Beginning — Rarely contributes or responds to discussion.
Example Response: "I agree with the river choice, but I think flooding could eventually force the civilization to move inland."
