Teacher Command Center

STEM Nation is not just a lesson. It is a guided civilization simulation experience where students move through observation, responsibility, decision, survival, consequence, reflection, and growth.

Start Here — Teacher Orientation

The teacher serves as the facilitator of the civilization experience — not simply the lecturer. STEM Nation is designed as a guided inquiry and civilization simulation system. Students move through structured decision pathways while educators guide discussion, evidence, reflection, and consequence analysis.

Baseline Inquiry: Module 0 is intentionally framed as observation and orientation rather than an obvious pre-assessment. Students first observe environments, ask questions, and gather evidence before making final settlement decisions.

Essential Question & Enduring Understandings

Essential Question

How does geography influence survival, decision-making, power, and the long-term growth of civilization?

Enduring Understandings

  • Civilizations develop through interconnected systems.
  • Geography shapes opportunity, limitation, movement, and risk.
  • Survival decisions create long-term consequences.
  • Observation and inquiry are foundational to leadership.

Week 1 Lesson Plan — Module 0: Cartography & Orientation

Students move from observation into responsibility and settlement defense. The goal is not simply choosing land, but defending a founding decision using evidence from the environment.

  • Students move through observation before final settlement defense.
  • Students generate questions and evidence from the environment.
  • Students complete the Strategic Map Defense.
  • Students create a Founding Archive artifact.
  • Teacher facilitates discussion around geography, sustainability, consequence, and leadership.
Suggested pause points: after observation, before settlement defense, and during archive review.
Week 1 currently uses the active Academy pathway rather than the older region_experience pathway. The Academy sequence is the locked instructional foundation for the current build.

Week 2 Lesson Plan — Survival & Consequence

Students now confront survival pressure. The focus shifts from choosing land to surviving on it through labor organization, resource management, and risk response.

  • Students create a first survival plan.
  • Students address food, shelter, scarcity, and labor.
  • Students export or print a Survival Archive.
  • Teacher facilitates tradeoff and consequence discussion.
Strong discussion prompt: “What causes civilizations to fail even when they choose strong land?”
Teachers are encouraged to pause frequently for student-generated questions, debate, and consequence prediction before students finalize survival responses.

Artifact Collection Workflow

  • Students can export text archives locally.
  • Students can print or Save-as-PDF from printable archive pages.
  • Teachers can collect archives digitally or physically.
  • Suggested naming convention: StudentName_Week1_FoundingArchive.pdf
  • Suggested naming convention: StudentName_Week2_SurvivalArchive.pdf
Current prototype saves locally in the browser using localStorage. Teacher cloud collection systems are planned for future phases.

Assessment & Reflection

Evidence & Reasoning

Does the student support decisions using observations from the environment?

Risk Awareness

Does the student recognize tradeoffs, dangers, and long-term consequences?

Systems Thinking

Does the student connect geography, survival, labor, and sustainability together?

Reflection

Can the student explain why civilizations succeed or fail?

Implementation Notes

  • Week 1 and Week 2 student routes are currently locked and stable.
  • The current prototype uses browser-based local saves.
  • Printable archives support teacher portfolio collection.
  • The system is designed for classroom, afterschool, and intensive implementation models.
  • Future phases will include teacher dashboards, cloud saves, scoring systems, and live civilization simulation layers.
