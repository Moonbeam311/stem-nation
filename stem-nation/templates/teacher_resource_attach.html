Resource-to-Simulation Attachment

Teachers should eventually be able to attach evidence, primary sources, media, maps, and instructional material directly to simulation moments inside STEM Nation.

Core Instructional Idea

Resources are not simply stored in a library. They are attached to specific learning moments where students encounter evidence, inquiry prompts, historical context, or discussion material during the simulation.

Simulation Attachment Targets

Module 0 / Baseline Inquiry

Attach maps, images, migration data, or environmental evidence to student observation stages.

Strategic Map Defense

Attach supporting evidence for settlement reasoning, geography, trade, and sustainability discussion.

Week 2 Survival Workspace

Attach first-hand accounts, environmental records, or survival-based inquiry material.

Future Governance Modules

Attach speeches, laws, treaties, debates, and public records to future civics/governance systems.

Student-Facing Experience

When students reach a lesson stage, attached resources may appear as:

  • Evidence cards
  • Primary-source prompts
  • Teacher-added context notes
  • Media references
  • Discussion prompts
  • Archive support materials
Goal: transform the simulation into an evidence-driven civilization inquiry system.
