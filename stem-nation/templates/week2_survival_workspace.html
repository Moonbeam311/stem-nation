Survival Mission Brief

You cannot do everything at once. Early civilizations survive by choosing priorities. Your job is to create a first survival plan that explains how your people will stay alive long enough to grow.

Survival Reference Board

Food Pressure

Question: What will people eat first?

Tradeoff: gathering is fast, farming is slower, hunting has risk.

Shelter Pressure

Question: What protects people immediately?

Tradeoff: quick shelters may be weak; strong shelters take labor.

Labor Pressure

Question: Who does what?

Tradeoff: every worker assigned to one task is unavailable for another.

Scarcity Pressure

Question: What happens when resources are limited?

Tradeoff: unfair distribution can weaken trust and unity.

Risk Pressure

Question: What danger comes first?

Tradeoff: ignoring risk may save time now but cost more later.

Consequence Pressure

Question: What will this choice make possible or impossible?

Tradeoff: survival choices shape future growth.
