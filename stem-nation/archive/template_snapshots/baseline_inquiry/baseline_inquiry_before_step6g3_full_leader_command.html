The Washed-Out Crossing

Academy Readiness Evaluation — Survival Scenario 1: Leader Command Trial

Your group was traveling along an old route when rising water and mud loosened the riverbank. The crossing behind you became unsafe. Part of the path washed out, and the group cannot safely return before dark.

The group has 12 people: four adults, three older youth, two young children, two elders, and one injured traveler. You are the acting field leader.

1. Leader’s First Questions

Before giving orders, what must be known?

2. Assign Materials to Specialists

Scout

Rope, marker stones, torn map scrap.

Builder

2 shelter cloths, 12 wooden stakes, hand axe, digging tool.

Gatherer

3 food sacks, 4 water jars, small tools.

Caretaker

2 blankets, fire starter / match tin, water jars.

3. Specialist Reports

Scout Report

The torn map scrap shows a broken river line, one old trail mark, a hill symbol, three faded tree marks, an X near the riverbend, and the partial warning “FLO…”

Builder Report

The river area is flat but wet. Stakes may not hold well. High ground is drier but rocky.

Gatherer Report

The river has water and fish. Food sacks near low ground may get wet. Food near the forest may attract animals.

Caretaker Report

The children, elders, and injured traveler need dry rest. Blankets and matches must stay dry.

4. Main Survival Question

5. Place Survival Bundles

Home Place

2 shelter cloths, 2 blankets, 12 stakes, fire starter if kept dry.

Food Supplies

3 food sacks, small tools, protected storage bundle.

Water Place

4 water jars, clean-water point, carrying route.

Path Marker

40 ft rope, 10 marker stones, torn map scrap.

6. Reflect / Continue

Your first plan does not have to be perfect. Leadership improves through evidence and revision.
