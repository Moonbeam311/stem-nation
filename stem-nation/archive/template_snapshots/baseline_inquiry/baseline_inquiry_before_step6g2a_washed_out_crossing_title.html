Academy Readiness Evaluation

Survival Scenario 1: Leader Command Trial
Time until nightfall: 40 minutes

The crossing behind you is gone.

Your group was traveling along an old route when rising water and mud loosened the riverbank. The crossing behind you became unsafe. Part of the path washed out, and the group cannot safely return before dark.

You have reached unfamiliar land with only 40 minutes before nightfall. The group has 12 people: four adults, three older youth, two young children, two elders, and one injured traveler.

The youngest are tired. The elders are moving slowly. The injured traveler needs rest. Some supplies are damp. The water jars are nearly empty.

You are the acting field leader. That does not mean you know all the answers. It means you must organize the questions, assign materials to specialists, listen to reports, weigh risks, and make the first survival decision before nightfall.

Why you cannot go backThe river crossing behind the group is unsafe. Mud, rising water, and fading daylight make return too dangerous before nightfall.
Who depends on you12 people: 4 adults, 3 older youth, 2 young children, 2 elders, and 1 injured traveler.
Your leadership jobAsk what must be known, assign materials, send specialists, read reports, and decide what risk to accept.
Survival needsWater, food or energy, air, space, shelter, warmth, safety, health, and suitable conditions.

Leader’s First Questions

Before giving orders, decide what you need to know first. The quality of your question may shape whether your group survives the first night.

River RiskIs the river rising, and could it flood the low ground before morning?
Safe RestWhere is the safest dry ground for children, elders, and the injured traveler to rest?
Food ProtectionWhere can food be protected from both water and animals?
Water AccessHow can the group reach water without sleeping on dangerous low ground?
MovementWhat path can be marked before dark so no one gets lost?
