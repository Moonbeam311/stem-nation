The Washed-Out Crossing

Academy Readiness Evaluation — Survival Scenario 1: Leader Command Trial
Nightfall: 40 minutes

Incident Briefing

Your group was traveling along an old route when rising water and mud loosened the riverbank. The crossing behind you became unsafe. Part of the path washed out, and the group cannot safely return before dark.

You have reached unfamiliar land with only 40 minutes before nightfall.

The group has 12 people: four adults, three older youth, two young children, two elders, and one injured traveler.

The youngest are tired. The elders are moving slowly. The injured traveler needs rest. Some supplies are damp. The water jars are nearly empty.

You are the acting field leader.

That does not mean you know all the answers.

It means you must organize the questions, assign materials to specialists, listen to reports, weigh risks, and make the first survival decision before nightfall.

Leadership Pressure
You are not being asked to guess. You are being asked to lead under uncertainty. Your first duty is to protect the most vulnerable people, preserve the materials that cannot be replaced, and ask the question that matters most before nightfall.
What can go wrong tonight?
Food can spoil. Matches can get wet. Elders and children can become exhausted. A wrong path can separate the group. Low ground can flood. Animals may follow food scent near the forest edge.
Why you cannot go backThe crossing is unstable, the riverbank is muddy, and daylight is fading.
What is at stakeWater, food, shelter, warmth, safety, health, and movement.
