Incident Briefing

Your group was traveling along an old route when rising water and mud loosened the riverbank. The crossing behind you became unsafe. Part of the path washed out, and the group cannot safely return before dark.

You have reached unfamiliar land with only 40 minutes before nightfall.

The group has 12 people: four adults, three older youth, two young children, two elders, and one injured traveler.

The youngest are tired. The elders are moving slowly. The injured traveler needs rest. Some supplies are damp. The water jars are nearly empty.

You are the acting field leader.

That does not mean you know all the answers.

It means you must organize the questions, assign materials to specialists, listen to reports, weigh risks, and make the first survival decision before nightfall.

GROUP MOMENT
A child asks quietly: “Will we still be walking when it gets dark?”
Tradeoff Doctrine
No survival plan removes all danger. Every decision protects something while risking something else.

Assign Materials

Issue Role Orders Before Assigning Materials Your priorities tell you what matters. Now turn those priorities into orders for each specialist. Then give each specialist the materials needed to carry out that order.
